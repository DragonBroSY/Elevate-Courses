## Regulations

- FAR: 50 titles; Title 14 is Aeronautics and Space
- CFR: 5 volumes, chapters and subchapters

**Part 1**: Night — end of evening civil twilight and the beginning of morning civil twilight as published in the air almanac, converted to local time

**Part 61**: Student pilot — Subpart C; Private pilot — Subpart E
- 61.113 — P-SCRIPT: Pro Rata Share, Sales, Charity, Recovery Ops, Incidental to business, Production testing, towing gliders
- 61.2: valid and current; 61.3: pilot certificate, ID, medical

**Medical**:
- 1st class (ATP): 12 months, <40 yrs old
- 1st class never downgrades — only the class privilege

**BasicMed**: driver's license, must have held valid FAA med after 2006; GW <12,500 lbs, <250 kts, not in Flight Levels; fill out CMEC; physician fills his part; ed course every 24 months

- 61.51: logbooks
- 61.56: flight review
- 61.57: recent flight experience (night: 3 T/O & landings, 1 hr before sunrise)
- 61.87 / 61.93: before solo requirements
- 61.103–113: pilot privileges and limitations

**Part 91** (all the ways to lose your license):
- 91.17: alcohol or drugs (Zyrtec, Dramamine, Benadryl, etc.)
- 91.113: right of way — see and avoid. Converging → least maneuverable to most maneuverable; overtaking; landing (lower alt a/c has right of way)
- 91.151: fuel requirement — 30 min reserve day; 45 min reserve night

**NTSB 830**: Accident = people on board seriously hurt, loss of life, or a/c seriously damaged; Incident = [eCFR 49 CFR Part 830](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-49/subtitle-B/chapter-VIII/part-830)

Advisory Circulars 0045H — no binding regulatory force, but de facto standards

FAA Letter of Interpretation: helps clarify true meaning of regulation ("chief counsel opinions")

## Airworthiness Requirements

- 14 CFR Part 91.3 — PIC has final authority
- 91.203 — airworthiness & registration certificate required: **ARROWPEC** (Airworthiness, Registration, Radio, Operator Supplement, W&B, Placards, Exterior Data Plate, Compass Deviation Card)

**Required Inspections — AViATE**:
- Annual
- VOR — 30 days
- 100-hour
- Altimeter/Static/Encoder — 24 months
- ELT — 12 months (121.5)

- **AD**: FAA "recall" — emergency or recurring (e.g. Cessna ignition wire ~100 hr)
- **Service Bulletin**: not mandatory; manufacturer notifies owners of improvements (mx blue binder)
- **Special Flight Permit**: not airworthy but safe for flight: fly to mx, deliver, testing — forms from FSDO

**Required Equipment**:

Day VFR — **ATOMATOFLAMES**: Altimeter, Tach, Oil pressure, Manifold, Airspeed, Temp, Oil temp, Fuel gauge, Landing gear indicator, Anti-collision, Mag compass, ELT, Safety belts

Night VFR — Day + **FLAPS**: Fuses, Landing light, Anti-collision, Position lights, Source of power

Flying with inop equipment: 91.213 — VFR day type cert, KOEL, 91.205, AD; removed or deactivated, placarded "INOP" per FAR 91.405

- STC is like a "marriage cert" — changing something inside
- Pilot-performed preventive mx: Part 43 Appendix A, 14 CFR 43.7 & 43.11

## Ground Reference Maneuvers

- Increase GS (tailwind) → increase bank; decrease GS (headwind) → decrease bank
- Entry: 600–1,000 AGL; use 1,000 AGL
- Clearing turn always left first — since right of way → someone could be passing on right
