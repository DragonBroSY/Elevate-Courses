Lift = Bernoulli & Newton's 3rd law — lift and weight can be equal at any speed by adjusting AOA

Planform is the shape of wing viewed from above: aspect ratio and tapering

**Drag**:
1. Parasite (nothing we can do) — Form, Interference, Skin friction
2. Induced (byproduct, "tax") — wingtip vortices (high pressure air from bottom of wing taking path of least resistance at the outboard edge)

**Ground Effect**: interference of ground surface with airflow (use float to bleed off speed, or soft-field TO)

**Wake turbulence**: down and out → rotate prior to other a/c, landing stay above and land beyond "bowl"

Air density affected by: pressure, temp, humidity

Forces during climb entry — lift is initially greater

Forces during descent — decrease AOA, weight initially greater than lift

Forces in turns: horizontal component = centrifugal force; slip turn — left turn, right rudder

**Moment**: measure of a/c tendency to rotate — *moment arm* = dist from reference point

**Static stability**: initial tendency back to equilibrium — positive / neutral / negative

- Longitudinal stability = pitch stability
- Lateral stability = roll stability
- **Dihedral**: upward angle formed by wing
- **Sweepback**: in a sideslip, low wing's leading edge is more perpendicular to relative airflow
- **Directional stability**: stability about the vertical axis = yaw stability, achieved by CG placement and sweepback

**Maneuvering Speed (Va)**: safe stalling speed; safe to make one full control deflection without damaging a/c

**Why stay within CG?** Stall Recovery! Center of Lift (CL) is aft of CG → a/c recovers from stalls itself. We do: reduce AOA, Max Power, Level wings

**Spin** = stall + yaw

- **PARE** for C172: Power-idle, Aileron-neutral, Rudder-Opposite, Elevator-full fwd
- **REAP** for Archer: Rudder-opposite, Elevator-full fwd, Aileron-neutral, Power-idle

**Prop principles**: TQ reaction (left roll), corkscrew effect (spiralling slipstream → left yaw), gyro precession (reaction at 90° later in direction of rotation → left yaw), asymmetric loading (P-factor → left yaw, bite of descending blade > bite of ascending blade)

Load factor increases exponentially after 60°

**Arm and station**: arm = dist from datum in inches; station = dist from datum with no units; datum is zero; moment is measured in torque

- Std empty weight = a/c + engine + non-drain oil + unusable fuel
- Basic empty weight = std empty + avionics, installed equipment
- Payload = occupants, cargo, baggage, cases of oil, chocks, etc.
- Zero fuel weight = everything but usable fuel
