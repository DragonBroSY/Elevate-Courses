*Ref: FAA Aviation Weather Handbook*

Stability is determined by lapse rate 2°/1,000ft — if <2°/1K: stable; if >2°/1K: unstable

- Unstable air: uplifting air; stable air: descending air
- Cool dry air = stable; warm and moist = unstable

**Dew point**: temp at which air can't hold more moisture → cloud forms, fog, dew, frost, rain, snow

**Cloud classification**:
- Low, middle, high
- Fluffiness: cirrus, stratus, cumulus (with vertical dev)
- Rime ice is "rough"
- Icing conditions: moisture + temp (+2°C to −20°C)

Sufficient moisture + atmospheric instability + lifting action = **thunderstorm** (anvil shaped) — stay 20nm from these

**AWOS/ASOS**: automated surface weather observation, on the field, minimum update

**METAR**: MET = Meteorological, A = Aviation, R = Routine Report — every hour or half-hour (5 min before the hour), unless special wx occurred (SPECI)

**PIREP** — [AOPA PIREP guide](https://www.aopa.org/training-and-safety/online-learning/safety-spotlights/pireps-made-easy/elements-of-a-pirep)

NEXRAD is up to 8 min behind

**TAF** (vs. MOS): forecast within 5nm of airport, good for 24hrs — issued 7th day of the month, at 1720Z

**AIRMET**: stuff en route
- Sierra: mountain obscuration, IFR conditions
- Tango: moderate turbulence
- Zulu: icing and freezing level

**SIGMET**: significant meteorological info, more serious than AIRMET
- Non-convective — DATI: Dust, Ash, Turbulence, Icing (not associated with thunderstorms)
- Convective — SHELT: Surface wind >50 kts, Hail >3/4", Embedded TS, Line of TS (squall), Tornadoes. Valid for four hours.
